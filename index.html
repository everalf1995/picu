HELPING RESIDENTS

LEARN AND GROW

PICU Passport is an iOS app created to enhance Resident Education in the Pediatric Intensive Care Unit (PICU)

How it works!

Confidential Information: Do not distribute

Easier than pen and paper

The PICU Passport app was created to facilitate learning for residents in the Pediatric Intensive Care Unit, by having all the tools necessary for their growth in one place.

Adding faculty into the equation

The app allows faculty to check residents' progress and the fields in which they might need more training, and empowers residents to ask faculty for specific instruction.

Filling gaps in knowledge

PICU Passport anonymously collects the progress of all residents, and allows designated users to access it to understand the effectiveness of residents' educational experiences in the PICU.

RESIDENTS

The PICU Passport app offers residents a simple checklist, alongside other tools to help residents keep track of procedural exposures and help them maintain specific learning goals for the rotation.

Reference Sheet

Allows users to check the dosage for the administration of drugs and the course of action during a medical procedure.

Checklist

Allows residents to keep track of the procedures and concepts they have yet to encounter.

Topic Guide

Gives in-depth explanation for the topics listed on the checklist and the skills a resident must acquire.

Question Management

Provides administrators a means to acquire more information from users to improve PICU training.

User Search

Allows faculty and administrators to check the progress of individual residents, helping them enhance their training.

Statistics

Gives administrators a way to understand where residents are standing on their PICU training.

User Guide

Residents:

Checking off a requirement

To check off a completed item, click the switch beside the topic you were exposed to. An item is complete when it turns blue.

Faculty:

Finding a resident's progress

Click on user search. Type the name or email of the resident you are looking for. Once you find them, click their name. On their profile, you can scroll through their checklist to see their progress. Completed items will have a blue checkmark next to them.

Admin:

Adding a question

Click on Add Question. Tap the top text to edit the question. Add as many answers as you want and submit when you are finished.

Viewing survey statistics

Click on Statistics. To view a general overview, select the survey and click Survey Overview. To view individual responses, select a survey and Individual Responses.

All Users:

Viewing reference material

On the home page, click the information bubble on the screen to open choices for reference material. When logged into an account, select the menu on the top right and select one of the options that appears.

Changing password or email

Log in to your account. Click the menu in the top left, and select Settings. Tap on the email or password section to change your settings

The application shall be an iOS application.
The application must be able to offer survey to users.
Users must be able to sign-in using a login and password.
Supervising faculty and residents must be able to create an account if they are a new user.
Residents must be able to submit their survey once completed.
Supervising faculty must be able to view resident checklists and surveys.
Supervising faculty must be able to search for residents by name.
A central database must be able to contain a master account list.
The user’s password must be matched against the passwords in the master account list.
User account must be able to contain an email, password, rotation dates, and user type.
Database must be able to hold all users in 7 service areas.
Residents must be able to view and interact with their checklist.
Residents must be able to save their current checklist progress.
Supervising Faculty - a staff member that is charge of monitoring resident progress
Resident - a recent graduate who is training at the teaching hospital to gain hands-on experience
Administrator - a user that is authorized to view survey data and add questions to the database
Survey - a form given to residents and faculty before and after their use of the app to gauge effectiveness
Login - a combination of a email and password
Register - a page that asks for account information and creates a new account
Email - a unique mailing address for the user to sign in with
Password - a sequence of characters that the user sets to sign into their account
Account information - email, password, and name
Resident query - a search involving either a resident's email or name, to find them in the database
Database - an online storage area for app data
Master account list - a list of all user accounts
Rotation - the time period that the resident will be in the hospital
Account type - Resident, faculty, and administrator classifiers
Requirement list - the topics a resident needs to learn about during their rotation
Requirement status - finished or not finished; the current state of a requirement item
Query results - the list of residents returned from a search
Topic guide - a list of the required topics and a description of how faculty could teach residents about that topic
Contact list - a list of emails that the user can reference if they need to ask someone a question
How to use page - a page that gives the user a summary of how to use the application
Reference sheet - a list of medications and their dosages for quick reference
Settings - a page that allows the user to change their email and password
Pre-survey - a survey that residents and faculty take after signing up for their account to establish a baseline
Post-survey - a survey that residents receive a day before their rotation ends and faculty receives six months after sign up to determine how much the PICU Passport has helped them
